Applying the lessons to a data platform context
In the last section of the book, Dave goes through the example of an ML process, and describes how his principles can be applied. I agree that the principles are generic and flexible across any software, which is one reason why this book is great.
In this short article, I will muse on how the principles can be made relevant in the context of data platform engineering and operations.
A data platform is surely a piece of software, and so of course all of the content applies in some way. However, I think there are enough differences between a data ecosystem and a pure 'software solution' to warrant some specific discussion about how to practically bring things to life.
The first thing to call out is the huge dependancy on human actors in most/all data platforms.
- Where is the boundary between software and human process?
